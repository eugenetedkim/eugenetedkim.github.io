My Story

At the age of 20, during the financial crisis of 2007-2008, I paused my university attendance to explore the workforce and myself. Despite the increasing unemployment rate, I started working full-time in logistics and at various companies within the supply chain over the next 7 years. From there, I got a good look at international trade, commerce, and myself.

At the age of 28, I put the workforce on pause, and in 2016 I received an associate degree in business from Chaffey College. My next plan was to pursue accounting in hopes of complementing my work experience, but I took a drastic turn. Instead, I went towards software engineering where I would really be able to prepare myself for the future job market.

I am majoring in Computer Science at California State University, San Bernardino and will graduate next summer. I am currently experimenting with web and mobile technologies to explore how it can improve our lives and to see how it can be used to express who I am, what I know, and how I can help contribute to our world. I aspire to become a great software engineer and very excited for the future.
